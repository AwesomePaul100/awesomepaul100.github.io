What I Do

Research Focus

Bridging the gap between hardware performance and human learning outcomes.

AI-Driven Micro-Credentialing & Remediation

As lead technical GRA on a 5-year NSF IUSE:HSI grant (EES-1953606), I design and deploy AI systems that automatically tag assessment items with micro-credentials, identify learner knowledge gaps, and generate personalized remediation pathways — deployed live on UCF's Canvas LMS via the AchieveUp framework.

Machine Learning NLP Canvas LMS Educational Data Mining NSF Grant

FPGA Acceleration of ML Workloads

I investigate hardware acceleration techniques using FPGAs and HLS (High-Level Synthesis) for low-footprint text classification tasks, enabling efficient on-device inference for edge educational systems. Recent work explores compression-based feature clustering targeting resource-constrained FPGA platforms.

FPGA HLS / Vitis Text Classification LLM Prompting Hardware Design

Agent-Based & Computational Social Science

I apply agent-based modeling (ABM) to predict student performance, simulate optimal remedial staffing, and model the dynamics of learner ecosystems. This cross-disciplinary work bridges ECE, education research, and computational social science — including a Best Poster Award at CSSSA 2024.
